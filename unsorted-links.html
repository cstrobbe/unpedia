  • Elon Musk Accidentally Reveals His "SECRET HACK" In An Interview (Video Advice on YouTube, 7 minutes, 20.01.2021).
    Elon Musk has achieved several things that were previously deemed impossible. This is because he uses first-principles thinking instead of reasoning by analogy. A first principle is a basic assumption that cannot be deduced any further. Instead of starting from assumptions, one should ask questions such as “What are we absolutely sure is true?” and &lduo;What has been proven?” One thing Musk has used this on is the production of batteries, more specifically on how to produce them more cheaply.
    First-principles thinking requires that you start from facts and that you make plans to improve the key parts. Instead of projecting the current form forward, one should project the function forward and abandoning the form. Most people do the opposite and ask, for example, why we do't have flying cars without realising that airplanes fulfil that function, i.e. transportation by flight.
    The video uses Gutenberg's invention of the printing press as an example; the wine press and movable type already existed in Gutenberg's time.

Procrastination Versus Productivity / Effectiveness

  • How to Avoid Procrastination: A Complete Guide (The Art of Improvement on YouTube, 8 minutes, 07.02.2021).
    After a four-minute introduction, the video presents four steps. Step 1: acknowledge your fear, i.e. identify why a specific task or project is stressing you out. Step 2: establish why you want to stop procrastinating and what the consequences will be when you keep procrastinating. Step 3: remove distractions. Step 4: take immediate action: as soon as you notice you start procrastinating, make sure you start working on your task, even if it is only for five minutes.
