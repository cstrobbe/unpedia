Nobel Prize in Literature

Nobel Prize Laureates

See List of Nobel laureates in Literature. The table in the Wikipedia article List of Nobel laureates in Literature lists several genres in which a laureate is or was active; the table below lists only the genres that were explicitly mentioned in the “citation”.

Laureates and Why They Were Awarded the Prize
Year Author Language Country Genre(s) Rationale Notes
1901 Sully Prudhomme French France poetry in special recognition of his poetic composition, which gives evidence of lofty idealism, artistic perfection and a rare combination of the qualities of both heart and intellect
1902 Theodor Mommsen German Germany history the greatest living master of the art of historical writing, with special reference to his monumental work A History of Rome
1903 Bjørnstjerne Bjørnson Norwegian Norway poetry as a tribute to his noble, magnificent and versatile poetry, which has always been distinguished by both the freshness of its inspiration and the rare purity of its spirit
1904 Frédéric Mistral Provençal France poetry, philology in recognition of the fresh originality and true inspiration of his poetic production, which faithfully reflects the natural scenery and native spirit of his people, and, in addition, his significant work as a Provençal philologist
1904 José Echegaray Spanish Spain drama in recognition of the numerous and brilliant compositions which, in an individual and original manner, have revived the great traditions of the Spanish drama
1905 Henryk Sienkiewicz Polish Poland / Russian Empire novel because of his outstanding merits as an epic writer (historical novels)
1906 Giosuè Carducci Italian Italy poetry not only in consideration of his deep learning and critical research, but above all as a tribute to the creative energy, freshness of style, and lyrical force which characterize his poetic masterpieces Carducci was also a literary critic and teacher.
1907 Rudyard Kipling English United Kingdom novel, short story, poetry in consideration of the power of observation, originality of imagination, virility of ideas and remarkable talent for narration that characterize the creations of this world-famous author
1908 Rudolf Christoph Eucken Germany Germany philosophy in recognition of his earnest search for truth, his penetrating power of thought, his wide range of vision, and the warmth and strength in presentation with which in his numerous works he has vindicated and developed an idealistic philosophy of life Eucken did not write any fiction.
1909 Selma Lagerlöf Swedish Sweden novel, short story in appreciation of the lofty idealism, vivid imagination and spiritual perception that characterize her writings
1910 Paul von Heyse German Germany as a tribute to the consummate artistry, permeated with idealism, which he has demonstrated during his long productive career as a lyric poet, dramatist, novelist and writer of world-renowned short stories poetry, drama, novel, short story
1911 Maurice Maeterlinck French Belgium drama in appreciation of his many-sided literary activities, and especially of his dramatic works, which are distinguished by a wealth of imagination and by a poetic fancy, which reveals, sometimes in the guise of a fairy tale, a deep inspiration, while in a mysterious way they appeal to the readers' own feelings and stimulate their imaginations (poetry, essay)
1912 Gerhart Hauptmann German Germany drama primarily in recognition of his fruitful, varied and outstanding production in the realm of dramatic art (novel)
1913 Rabindranath Tagore Bengali, English India (British Empire) poetry because of his profoundly sensitive, fresh and beautiful verse, by which, with consummate skill, he has made his poetic thought, expressed in his own English words, a part of the literature of the West (novel, drama, short story, music, essay, philosophy, literary criticism, translation)
1915 Romain Rolland French France novel as a tribute to the lofty idealism of his literary production and to the sympathy and love of truth with which he has described different types of human beings
1916 Verner von Heidenstam Swedish Sweden (poetry, novel) in recognition of his significance as the leading representative of a new era in our literature
1917 Karl Adolph Gjellerup Danish, German Denmark poetry for his varied and rich poetry, which is inspired by lofty ideals
1917 Henrik Pontoppidan Danish Denmark (novel) for his authentic descriptions of present-day life in Denmark
1919 Carl Spitteler German Switzerland poery in special appreciation of his epic, Olympian Spring
1920 Knut Hamsun Norwegian Norway novel for his monumental work, Growth of the Soil
1921 Anatole France French France (novel, poetry) in recognition of his brilliant literary achievements, characterized as they are by a nobility of style, a profound human sympathy, grace, and a true Gallic temperament
1922 Jacinto Benavente Spanish Spain drama for the happy manner in which he has continued the illustrious traditions of the Spanish drama
1923 William Butler Yeats English Ireland poetry for his always inspired poetry, which in a highly artistic form gives expression to the spirit of a whole nation Yeats's plays are not mentioned
1924 Władysław Reymont Polish Poland novel for his great national epic, The Peasants
1925 George Bernard Shaw English Ireland (drama) for his work which is marked by both idealism and humanity, its stimulating satire often being infused with a singular poetic beauty (literary criticism)
