Renaissance / Elizabethan Prose

  • Hadfield, Andrew (editor): The Oxford Handbook of English Prose 1500-1640. Oxford University Press, 2016. (768 pages) ISBN 9780198778349.
  • Salzman, Paul (editor): An Anthology of Elizabethan Prose Fiction. Oxford University Press, 2008. (464 pages) ISBN 9780199540570.
  • Walton, Izaak; Cotton, Charles: The Compleat Angler. Edited by Marjorie Swann. Oxford University Press, 2016. (336 pages) ISBN 9780198745464.
  • Mentz, Steve: Romance for Sale in Early Modern England: The Rise of Prose Fiction. Routledge, 2006. (272 pages) ISBN 9781138250925.
  • Davis, Alex: Renaissance Historical Fiction: Sidney, Deloney, Nashe. Studies in Renaissance Literature. Boydell and Brewer, 2011. (264 pages) ISBN 9781843842682.
    From the publisher's description: A majority of the fiction composed in England in the second half of the sixteenth century was set in the past. All the major prose writers of the period (Thomas Lodge, Sir Philip Sidney, Thomas Nashe, Thomas Deloney, Robert Greene) produced historical fiction, with settings ranging from the ancient world (as in Sidney's Arcadia) to the time of Henry VIII (in Nashe's The Unfortunate Traveller). Yet while studies of the historical drama of the period abound, the historical bias of prose fiction has so far escaped any sort of sustained critical consideration.
    See also the review by Elizabeth Rivlin (Renaissance Quarterly, 2011).

Cultural and Religious Background

  • Girouard, Mark: The best books on Art and Culture in Elizabethan England, Five Books (no date). Girouard recommends the following books:
    • English Literature in the Renaissance Excluding Drama by C. S. Lewis,
    • Art and Decoration in Elizabethan and Jacobean England by Anthony Wells-Cole,
    • The Embroideries at Hardwick Hall by Santina M. Levey,
    • Theatre of the World by Frances A. Yate and
    • Elinor Fettiplace's Receipt Book by Hilary Spurling.
  • Swift, Daniel: Shakespeare's Common Prayers: The Book of Common Prayer and the Elizabethan Age. Oxford University Press, 2012. ISBN 9780199838561.
  • Dobranski, Stephen: Readers and Authorship in Early Modern England. Cambridge University Press, 2009. (244 pages) ISBN 9780521120180.
  • King, John N.: Tudor Books and Readers: Materiality and the Construction of Meaning. Cambridge University Press, 2013. (290 pages) ISBN 9781107412552.
  • Salzman, Paul: Literary Culture in Jacobean England: Reading 1621. Palgrave, 2002. ISBN 978-1-349-50742-9.
    From the publisher's description: This book offers an unparalleled depth of historical research by surveying the extraordinary richness of literary culture in a single year. Paul Salzman examines what is written, published, performed and, in some cases, even spoken during 1621 in Britain.
  • Hackett, Helen: The best books on Elizabeth I, Five Books (no date). Hackett recommends the following books:
    • Elizabeth I: The Exhibition Catalogue, edited by David Starkey and Susan Doran,
    • The Faerie Queene by Edmund Spenser,
    • Translations by Elizabeth I, 1592 – 98, edited by Janel Muller and Joshua Scodel,
    • Rewriting the Renaissance by Margaret W. Ferguson, Maureen Quilligan and Nancy Vickers,
    • Representing Elizabeth in Stuart England by John A. Watkins.

Edmund Spenser

  • The Spenser Review: an online journal published three times a year (…). Sponsored [by] the International Spenser Society with support from The University of South Carolina and Washington University in St. Louis, the Review publishes both single reviews and essay-reviews of topics in and around Spenser studies.
  • Spenser Studies: A Renaissance Poetry Annual: a journal devoted to the study of Edmund Spenser and the poetry of his immediate context, early modern England.

Other Links

  • Renaissance and Reformation / Renaissance et Réforme: a peer-reviewed quarterly journal, multidisciplinary and bilingual.
  • Bruce, Susan; Steinberger, Rebecca (editors): The Renaissance Literature Handbook. Continuum, 2009. (248 pages) ISBN 9780826495006. (Companion website.)
  • Hackett, Helen: A Short History of English Renaissance Drama. Short Histories. I. B. Tauris, 2012. (256 pages) ISBN 9781848856585.
  • The Malone Society: an organisation devoted to textual scholarship of late-medieval and early modern drama. The society is named after Edmond Malone, who published one of the most significant eighteenth-century editions of Shakespeare's works.
