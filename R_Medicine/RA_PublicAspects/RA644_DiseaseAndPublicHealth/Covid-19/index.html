  • Coronavirus is not the flu. It's worse. (Vox on YouTube, 6 minutes, 01.04.2020).
    This video explains a number of important differences beteween influenza and Covid-19. First, the basic reproduction number (R0) for influenza is 1.3, whereas for Covid-19 it is 2–2.5 (for measles, it is 12–18), which makes a huge difference after, for example, 10 rounds of infections (56 versus 2047, respectively). Second, the incubation periods are very different: 2 days for influenza versus 5 to 14 days for Covid-19. So someone who has been infected will infect many more other people before they start feeling sick. Third, there always a number people who are immune to the seasonal flu, either because they have already had that strain of the virus or because they have had a flu shot. This limits the spread of the virus. The SARS-CoV-2 virus is new, so nobody is immune and everyone is susceptible. In addition, only 2% infected by the flu virus need to be hospitalised, whereas 20 to 31% of people with Covid-19 end up in hospital. Covid-19's fatality rate might be between 1% and 3% (this is still uncertain) but for flu it is 0.1%.
