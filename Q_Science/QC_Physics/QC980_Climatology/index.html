Books

  • Monbiot, George: Heat: How We Can Stop the Planet Burning. Penguin, 2007. (320 pages) ISBN 9780141026626.
    See also Monbiot's webpage about Heat.
  • Lynas, Mark: Six Degrees: Our Future on a Hotter Planet. London: Fourth Estate, 2007.
    See the Wikipedia article Six Degrees: Our Future on a Hotter Planet and 'Six Degrees: Our Future on a Hotter Planet,' Mark Lynas on OpenDemocracy.
  • Klein, Naomi: This Changes Everything: Capitalism vs. The Climate. Simon & Schuster, 2015. (576 pages) ISBN 9781451697391.
    See also the website This Changes Everything, where you can download a classroom guide for the book.
  • Maslin, Mark: Climate Change: A Very Short Introduction. Oxford University Press, 2014. (216 pages) ISBN 9780198719045.
  • Flannery, Tim: Atmosphere of Hope: Searching for Solutions to the Climate Crisis. Grove, 2016. (272 pages) ISBN 978-0-8021-2565-1.
    See also Atmosphere of Hope: Searching for Solutions to the Climate Crisis on the Climate Council website.
  • Marohasy, Jennifer (editor): Climate Change: The Facts 2017. Connor Court Publishing, August 2017. (380 pages) ISBN 9780909536039.
    From the publisher's description: Climate Change: The Facts 2017 contains 22 essays by internationally-renowned experts and commentators, including Dr Bjorn Lomborg, Dr Matt Ridley, Professor Peter Ridd, Dr Willie Soon, Dr Ian Plimer, Dr Roy Spencer, and literary giant Clive James. Dr Jennifer Marohasy said, “Climate Change: The Facts 2017 presents the case for climate change policies to be based on scientific evidence and it reveals how many of the potential policy responses to climate change are often wildly disproportionate compared to their potential cost.” Note that Connor Court Publishing has published several books that deny that climate change is human made etcetera.

Climate Change

  • Exxon Researched Climate Change in 1977, InsideClimate News (no date).
  • Banerjee, Neela; Song, Lisa; Hasemyer, David: Exxon: The Road not Taken, InsideClimate News, 16.09.2015.
  • Hall, Shannon: Exxon Knew about Climate Change almost 40 years ago, Scientific American, 26.10.2015.
    Exxon, which merged with Mobil in 1999, was aware of climate change as early as 1977.
  • Swiss National Science Foundation: Sun's impact on climate change quantified for first time, Phys.org, 27.07.2017.
    Researchers in Switzerland have developed elaborate model calculations [that] are supplying a robust estimate of the contribution that the sun is expected to make to temperature change in the next 100 years. For the first time, a significant effect is apparent. They expect the Earth's temperature to fall by half a degree when solar activity reaches its next minimum.
