Obesity

  • Christakis, Nicholas A.; Fowler, James H.: The Spread of Obesity in a Large Social Network over 32 Years, New England Journal of Medicine, 26.07.2007.
  • Cohen-Cole, Ethan; Fletcher, Jason M.: Is obesity contagious? Social networks vs. environmental factors in the obesity epidemic, Journal of Health Economics, volume 27, issue 5 (September 2008).
  • Winer, Shawn; et al: Normalization of obesity-associated insulin resistance through immunotherapy, Nature Medicine, August 2009.
  • Transmitting Obesity through Social Networks: The Underwhelming Influence of Social Norms, Networks Course blog for INFO 2040/CS 2850/Econ 2040/SOC 2090, 01.10.2011.
    Discusses the research findings by Christakis and Fowler (2007) and by Hruschka and others (2009).
  • Hruschka, Daniel J.; Brewis, Alexandra A.; Wutich, Amber; Morin, Benjamin: Shared norms and their explanation for the social clustering of obesity, American Journal of Public Health, December 2011.
  • Bartle, Naomi C.: Is Social Clustering Of Obesity Due To Social Contagion Or Genetic Transmission?, American Journal of Public Health, January 2012.
  • Musaiger, Abdulrahman O.; Al-Mannai, Mariam: Role of obesity and media in body weight concern among female university students in Kuwait, Eating Behaviors, April 2013.
  • Bruening, Meg; at al: Friendship as a social mechanism influencing body mass index (BMI) among emerging adults, PLoS One, 2018.
    Conclusion: Analyses indicated BMI affected friend selection, not through students selecting friends with similar BMI, but rather, by students avoiding friends with more extreme BMI levels.
  • Diabetes and Obesity, Diabetes.co.uk, 15.01.2019.
  • Measuring Obesity: From Calipers to CAT Scans, Ten Ways to Tell Whether a Body Is Fat or Lean, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health (no date, accessed on 27.05.2021).
    The methods on this page are BMI, waist circumference, waist-to-hip ratio, skinfold thickness, bioelectric impedance (BIA), underwater weighing (densitometry), air-displacement plethysmography, the dilution method (hydrometry), Dual Energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DEXA), Computerized Tomography (CT) and Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI).
  • Obesity Rates By Country 2021, World Population Review (no date; accessed on 27.05.2021).
  • Link between BMI and diabetes risk varies across the world, Diabetes.co.uk, 05.08.2021.
