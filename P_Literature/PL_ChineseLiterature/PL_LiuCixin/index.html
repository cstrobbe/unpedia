Remembrance of Earth's Past Trilogy

The Remembrance of Earth's Past trilogy (地球往事) consists of the following books:

  • Liu, Cixin: The Three-Body Problem. Original title: 三体 (重庆出版社, 2006, ISBN 978-7-5366-9293-0). Translated by Ken Liu. Head of Zeus, 2015. ISBN 978-1784971671.
    This was the first Asian novel to win the Hugo Award for Best Novel. (Wikipedia article.)
  • Liu, Cixin: The Dark Forest. Original title: 黑暗森林 (重庆出版社, 2008, ISBN 978-7-5366-9396-8). Translated by Joel Martinsen. Head of Zeus, 2015. ISBN 9781784971618. (Wikipedia article.)
  • Liu, Cixin: Death's End. Original title: 死神永生 (重庆出版社, 2010, ISBN 978-7-229-03093-3). Translated by Ken Liu. Head of Zeus, 2017. ISBN 9781784971656. (Wikipedia article.)

Ken Liu's Translation of The Three-Body Problem

Ken Liu's translation received a lot of praise. The differences between the translation have also drawn some attention; Ken Liu discussed his approach to the translation work in an afterword. One of the statements in this afterword has often been quoted, for example on Twitter: Overly literal translations, far from being faithful, actually distort meaning by obscuring sense.
