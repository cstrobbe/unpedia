Books about Lisp

  • Barski, Conrad: Land of Lisp. No Starch Press, 2010. (504 pages) ISBN 978-1-59327-281-4.
    See also the website for Land of Lisp.
  • Seibel, Peter: Practical Common Lisp. Apress, 2011. ISBN 978-1-4302-4290-1 (Springer).
    See also the site for the book Practical Common Lisp!
  • Penev, Pavel: Lisp Web Tales (e-book published by Learnpub, 2013).
  • Knott, Gary: Interpreting LISP: Programming and Data Structures. Apriss, 2017. ISBN 978-1-4842-2706-0 (Springer).
  • Weitz, Edmund: Common Lisp Recipes: A Problem-Solution Approach. Apress, 2016. ISBN 978-1-4842-1177-9 (Springer).
    See also the site for the book Common Lisp Recipes, with links to source code, errata and addenda.
  • Touretzky, David S.: Common Lisp: A Gentle Introduction to Symbolic Computation. Dover, 2013. (608 pages; reprint of the 1990 edition with minor revisions) ISBN 9780486498201.
    According to Touretzky's webapage for the book, the 1990 edition may be distributed for free for non-profit educational purposes.
  • Norvig, Peter: Paradigms of Artificial Intelligence Programming: Case Studies in Common Lisp. ISBN 978-0-08-057115-7.
    Lisp code for the book Paradigms of Artificial Intelligence Programming is available on GitHub (licence: MIT).
  • Kiczales, Gregor; des Rivières, Jim; Bobrow, Daniel G.: The Art of the Metaobject Protocol. MIT Press, 1991. ISBN 9780262610742.
    See also the C2 Wiki.
