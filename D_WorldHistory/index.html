History of the Ancient Near East

  • Podany, Amanda H.: The Ancient Near East: A Very Short Introduction. Oxford University Press, 2013. (168 pages) ISBN 9780195377996.
    This book covers the history of the Ancient Near East from around 3600 BCE to the end of the Neo-Babylonian empire in 539 BCE.
  • Oppenheim, Leo L.: Ancient Mesopotamia: Portrait of a Dead Civilization. Revised edition. University of Chicago Press, 1976. (462 pages) ISBN 9780226631875.
    According to Michael Jursa, this is with a wide margin the best general book on the topic.
  • Van De Mieroop, Marc: A History of the Ancient Near East, ca. 3000-323 BC. Third edition. Wiley Blackwell, 2015. (432 pages) ISBN 978-1-118-71816-2.
  • Jursa, Michael: Death and Taxes? Economy, Society and the Imperial State in Babylonia in the Sixth Century BCE, Institute for the Study of the Ancient World, New York University, 29.09.2016.
  • Elam, Encyclopaedia Iranica.
  • Assyria i: The Kingdom of Assyria and its Relations with Iran, Encyclopaedia Iranica, 15.12.1987, last updated on 07.07.2016.
  • Babylonia i: History of Babylonia in the Median and Achaemenid periods, Encyclopaedia Iranica, 15.12.1998, last updated on 19.08.2011.
  • Nimrud: Materialities of Assyrian Knowledge Production, Oracc: The Open Richly Annotated Cuneiform Corpus. (Content released under a CC BY-SA 3.0 licence, 2013-14.)
