Senses and Perception

We are usually taught that humans have five senses: sight, hearing, touch, taste and smell. This list goes back to Aristotle's De anima and is based on a list of sensory organs: eyes, ears, skin, mouth and nose. But sense is more complex than this. Other senses that humans have include thermoception, proprioception (also referred to as kinaesthesia), equilibrioception (sense of balance), chronoception (time perception) and nociception or nocioception (perception of noxious stimuli). However, not all of these are relevant to human-computer interaction or usability.
