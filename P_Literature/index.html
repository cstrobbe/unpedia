  • Bacon, Francis: New Atlantis (incomplete utopian novel, published in 1627).
  • London, Jack: The Iron Heel (1908).
  • Forster, E. M.: The Machine Stops (short story, 1909).
  • Gilman, Charlotte Perkins: Herland (1915).
  • Zamyatin, Yevgeni: We (completed in 1921, published in 1924).
  • Platonov, Andrei: The Foundation Pit (finished in 1930; first published in 1987).
  • Huxley, Aldous: Brave New World (1932).
  • Lewis, Sinclair: It Can't Happen Here (1935).
  • Čapek, Karel: War with the Newts (1936; “satirical science fiction novela”; selected for the UNESCO Collection of Representative Works).
  • Boye, Karin: Kallocain (1940).
  • Koestler, Arthur: Darkness at Noon (1940).
  • Barjavel, René: Ashes, Ashes (1943; French title: Ravage).
  • Skinner, B. F.: Walden Two (1948).
  • Orwell, George: Nineteen Eighty-Four (1949).
  • Bradbury, Ray: Fahrenheit 451 (1953).
  • Golding, William: Lord of the Flies (1954; source of inspiration: The Coral Island (1858) by R. M. Ballantyne).
  • Numa Shōzō: Yapou, bétail humain (serialised in 1956 – 1959, in Japanese).
  • Huxley, Aldous: Island (1962).
  • Burgess, Anthony: A Clockwork Orange (1962).
  • Levin, Ira: This Perfect Day (1970).
  • Callenbach, Ernest: Ecotopia (1975).
  • Atwood, Margaret: The Handmaid's Tale (1985).
  • Rufin, Jean-Christophe: Globalia (2004).

Text Collections and Archives

  • Perseus Digital Library: a digital library with collections of Greek and Roman materials, Arabic language documents, Germanic materials, Renaissance materials and 19th-century American materials.
  • The Virtual Library: a collection of texts that are in the public domain.
  • Project Gutenberg: collection of over 55,000 free e-books.
  • Representative Poetry Online (RPO): an anthology of poems in English and French by over 700 poets. This includes HTML versions of manuscripts of Geoffrey Chaucer's works.
  • The Digital Humanities Institute maintains website that include the following:
    • The Acts and Monuments Online: several editions of a work that is also known as the Book of Martyrs.
    • Manuscripts Online
    • The Hartlib Papers
    • Torquemada en la hoguera
    • The Sheffield Corpus of Chinese: texts from the Song (960-1279), Ming (1366-1644) and Qing (1644-1911) dynasties.
  • Textes édités à ATHENA (©): collection of texts in various languages (Latin, French, German and Spanish but mostly in French).
  • Sources littéraires - E-Textes - Quelques archives générales: links to archives of electronic texts.
