Scott Moncrief's decision to translate the book's title Remembrance of Things Past, conveys an idea that is really the opposite of Proust’s own. (…) Proust’s theory of memory rejects the notion that we can simply sit and quietly resurrect the past in its true vividness through what he called voluntary memory.

Jean Giono

  • Jean Giono, écrivain de la violence humaine (France Culture on YouTube, 4 minutes, 23.10.2019).
    Excerpts from an interview with novelist Emmanuelle Lambert, who published a book about Giono. Giono's work is often thought of as an example of régionnalisme, but the author rejected that type of literature. Lambert thinks of him more as an author about human violence.
  • Giono, le midi en partage (France Culture on YouTube, 27 minutes, 23.10.2019).
    Interview with novelist Emmanuelle Lambert, author of Giono, furioso (Stock, 2019).
  • Citron, Pierre: Giono (1895-1970). Seuil, 1990. (672 pages) ISBN 9782020122122.
