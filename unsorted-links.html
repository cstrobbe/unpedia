Effectiveness / Success

  • The 3 Secrets Of Success (In59seconds on YouTube, 59 seconds, 09.03.2015).
    Three points: make a pledge (tell other people what you're trying to do), created a detailed plan by breaking your goal into a series of smaller steps, plot your progress. Summary: pledge, plan and plot.
  • Schweitzer, Joe: How I tricked my brain to like doing hard things (Better Ideas on YouTube, 11 minutes (the last two minutes are an ad), 25.10.2019).
    Joe changed his mindset about doing things he finds boring or difficult by tricking his brain into liking these things: he stopped generating motivation by thinking about an end goal and instead learnt to enjoy the process. The downside to focusing on an end goal is that there is a big disparity between that goal and the effort needed to get there. In addition, that goal will also turn out to be a moving target. Finally, there is entropy: stagnation breeds destruction. This is why you need to learn to like the process and the daily discipline. This way, the process itself will generate its own motivation. This does not mean that having goals is bad: goals create direction.
    Joe gives two practical tips. First, you should operate from an abundance mindset rather than a scarcity mindset. For example, instead of telling yourself that you need to go to the gym to get jacked (scarcity mindset), tell yourself that you want to go to the gym because you are an active person and going to the gym is something that is important to you. When you tell yourself that going to the gym is just something you do, you get rid of a lot of mental resistance. Joe cites the musician John Mayer as someone who does this.
    Second, utilise habit bunching: pair an already existing habit with one that you are trying to work on. For example, Joe likes a cup of coffee in the morning and he parired this habit (or addiction) with reading: he placed the book he wanted to read next to the coffee machine, so he would pick it up when he made his coffee. Eventually, he got used to reading, even without coffee.

Tutorials

  • [76] How To Learn The Binding Order When Picking Locks (HelpfulLockPicker on YouTube, 12 minutes, 06.09.2017).
    This video explains what binding order is using two acrylic locks and a cutawa lock. The binding order is the order that we have to set the pins in order to get the lock open and its different on every lock. When a pin is binding, you should hear a click. (HelpfulLockPicker uses lower tension than the LockPickingLawyer, so the clicks are more difficult to hear.) On the first acrylic lock, some of the picks don't even need to get set. On the second lock, only half of the six pins need to be set. The second lock is a cutaway lock with a relatively tight keyway in which each of the six pins need to be set.
  • [188] My Approach to Lock Picking Tension (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 16 minutes, 18.06.2016).
    Tension is an underrated aspect of picking. The advice, “use the lightest tension possible” is too simplistic. A tension tool should be stiff, not flexible, so as to transmit feedback from the lock to the picker. Using top-of-the-keyway tension is that it leaves room for the picking tool. Bottom-of-the-keyway tension can be useful in big keyways, so you can use it as a platform for the pick.
    LPL's advice is to use the heaviest tension possible for each particular lock. Heavier tension provides clearer feedback, which makes it more obvious which pins are binding. It also has other benefits. Tension is not static.

  • [1160] “The Ultimate Portable Safe” Opened With Red Bull Can (SafeGo) (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 3 minutes, 05.09.2020).
    This is plastic safe with a cheap combination lock. It can be opened using a shim cut from a can.
  • [1272] Good Job Master Lock... Kinda. (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 2 minutes, 10.04.2021).
    This video shows a combination lock that relies on a combination of four letters. The good thing about the lock is that it can't be opened using a shim (unlike many other combination locks). However there is another bypass: push down lightly on the shackle and firmly strike on lock body with a rubber hammer.
  • [756] Sentry Safe Cut in Half FAST! (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 5 minutes, 28.09.2018).
    Instead of picking the lock, the LockPickingLawyer uses a low-skill method to open the safe: he uses a circular saw. This is an easy attack, since the metal outer layer is very thin and between the outer lawyer and the plastic inside, there is only a layer of fire insulation.
  • [1044] Opened With FORK: Vaultek LifePod Gun Safe (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 2 minutes, 14.02.2020).
    This portable gunsafe is innovative because it is waterproof. Unfortunately, it can be opened by inserting a fork through the gap under the cover to push the internal button that allows one to program a new access code. The safe can then be opened using the new combination.
    The safe's manufacturer posted a comment below the video explaining how they disabled this exploit: All Lifepods produced and shipping from now on are upgraded with new firmware to disable the program button when locked, and we are also providing a solution for our current Lifepod customers.
  • [374] Battalion 1XRV6 Padlock - The Lock That Would Not Stay Closed! (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 6 minutes, 30.01.2017).
    This is a Chinese padlock that looked promising but that can be opened with almost any lockpicking tool, even with a dimple pick, a “knife”, a broken key extractor and a tension wrench.

Other Locks

  • [192] IFAM Huno 80 Shutter Lock v. Chinese Copy: Pick, Gut, & Compare (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 11 minutes, 22.06.2016).
    The video compares the IFAM Huno 80 shutter lock with a cheap Chinese imitation. The Chinese lock can be easily raked using a dimple pick. The other lock cannot be opened by raking it, though it can be opened using single-pin picking, which requires more skill. The video then shows how locks like this can be disassembled.
  • [721] ERA “Viscount” 5-Lever Mortice Lock Picked (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 4 minutes, 25.07.2018).
  • [626] A Pick Proof Master Lock? ALMOST! (LockPickingLawyer on YouTube, 11 minutes, 24.02.2018).
    Master Lock No. 18 is very pick resistant (due to its reverse side bar) but still has other flaws, such as a vulnerability to shimming. The LockPickingLawyer first shows the lock's internals to show how it works (10 minutes) and picks it in 30 seconds.
