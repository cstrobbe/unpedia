Links About Close Reading

  • What is close reading? This question was posted on Literature Stack Exchange in March 2017. An example of the method can be seen in a close reading of two stanzas from Henry Reed's poem “The Naming of Parts”.
  • Wikipedia contributors: Close reading, Wikipedia.
  • The Close Reading of Poetry: A Practical Introduction and Guide to Explication. A resource created by Dr. G. Kim Blank and Dr. Magdalena Kay (University of Victoria, Canada).
  • Phelan, Joe: The Birth of Close Reading, Edsitement, 05.01.2015.
    The author claims that Understanding Poetry by obert Penn Warren and Cleanth Brooks is arguable that the best resource for the close reading of poetry ever written.
  • Tearly, Oliver: Close Reading: How to Read a Poem, Interesting Literature, 13.11.2015.
  • Close Reading. Steps for Close Reading or Explication de texte: Patterns, polarities, problems, paradigm, puzzles, perception, The LIterary Link (no date).
    In addition to providing a short guide, this text points out that close reading must be done before you can begin to appropriate any theoretical or specific literary approach.
  • Nissila, Phyllis: Lesson 3.4: Close Reading for Literature, How to Learn Like a Pro! (no date; Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License).
  • Nordquist, Richard: Quotes About Close Reading, ThoughtCo, 05.07.2019.
