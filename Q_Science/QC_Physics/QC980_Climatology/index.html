  • This Is Your Brain On Stale Air (Tom Scott on YouTube, 6 minutes, 21.01.2019).
    This is a guest video by Kurtis Baute, who talks about carbondioxide concentrations in the air and their effect on cognitive functioning. In natural environments, carbondioxide levels are now at around 300ppm; in cities, carbondioxide levels often reach around 400 ppm. Indoors, carbondioxide can reach levels of 1000 ppm. However, at 1000 ppm there is a decrease of 15% in cognitive function. In classrooms, carbondioxide levels of reach 1000 ppm. At 1400 ppm, there is a 50% decrease in cognitive function. At 2000 ppm, there are various side effects, such as mild nausea, loss of attention, headaches, sleepiness and an increased heartrate. In bedrooms and cars, carbondioxide levels often reach 4000 ppm. In motorcycle helmets, carbondioxide levels can also become dangerously high. Gobally, carbondioxide levels outdoors might reach 695 ppm by the end of the 21st century; as a consequence, we could end up with a 10-16% decrease in our cognitive function as a species.
