  • How Long The COVID-19 Coronavirus Lasts On Everyday Surfaces (Science Insider on YouTube, 3 minutes, 19.03.2020).
    SARS-CoV-2 is not airborne but can survive up to three hours in droplets in the air, on copper surfaces, the virus can survive up to four hours, on cardboard up to 24 hours (although it is unlikely that packages or mail can transmit the virus), on plastic and stainless steel the virus can survive two to three days (which is longer than the influenza virus). Researchers don't know yet how long the virus can survive on a phone or a phone case.
    To reduce the risk of exposure, do not touch your eyes, mouth and face, wash your hands with water and soap for at least 20 seconds, clean commonly used surfaces with disinfecting wipes and avoid non-essential travel to public places, especially at peak times.
