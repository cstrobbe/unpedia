Encyclopedias

General Encyclopedias

  • Wikipedia (homepage with links to various language versions); Wikipedia (English).
    You can get an off-line version of Wikpedia using Kiwix.
  • Citizendium.
  • Scholarpedia: the peer-reviewed open-access encyclopedia.
  • Wikipedia for Schools: a selection of articles from Wikipedia [that] matches the UK National Curriculum and can be used by school children around the world.
  • Encyclo: online encylopedias set up by an organisation in the Netherlands:
    • Encyclo.co.uk: English encyclopedia.
    • Enzyklo.de: deutsche Enzyklopädie.
    • Encyclo.nl: Nederlandse encyclopedie.
  • Wikisage: The free encyclopedia of the second generation.
  • Deletionpedia: a radical inclusionist wiki for rescuing articles from Wikipedia's deletionism.
  • Enciclopedia Libre Universal en Español: a fork of the Spanish Wikipedia.
  • The Encyclopaedia Britannica: A Dictionary of Arts, Sciences, Literature and General Information (11th edition), hosted on the The Online Books Page at the University of Pennsylvania Libraries.
  • Oxford Music Online.
  • CoinFactsWiki: the CoinFacts Numismatic Wiki, the on-line encyclopedia of U.S. and World coins.

See also Wikipedia's list of online encyclopedias.

Specialised Encyclopedias

  • Encyclopedia of Earth (EoE): an electronic reference about the Earth, its natural environments, and their interaction with society.
  • Encyclopedia of Life.
  • Vikidia: a French encyclopedia for children between eight and thirteen years of age.

Biased Encyclopedias

  • The Catholic Encyclopedia.
  • Conservapedia: an encyclopedia biased by American conservative ideas.
