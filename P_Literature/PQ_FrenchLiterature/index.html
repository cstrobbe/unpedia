Isabelle Eberhardt (1877 – 1904)

Isabelle Eberhardt was a Swiss explorer and author. The illegitimate daughter of a Russian anarchist and a member of the Russian aristocracy, she grew up in Switzerland, where she learnt French, German, Russian, Italian, Latin, Greek and classical Arabic. She began wearing male clothing at a young age, moved to Algeria in 1897, converted to Islam, married an Algerian soldier in 1901, survived an assassination attempt and died in a flash flood at the age of 27.
Posthumously, she was seen as an early advocate of feminism and decolonisation. Most of her writings were published after her death.
