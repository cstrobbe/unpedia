How to Read Literature

You cannot teach a person to read well unless you, and particularly if he has a fine intelligence to begin with, a native aptitude, unless you make him struggle with texts that are very difficult and over his head. (…) The person who is (…) reading, however widely it is, has dealt with readily intelligible stuff, stuff he doesn't have to struggle to master, struggle to understand, is a person whose mind will not be pulled up to its full capacity. (Mortimer J. Adler in an interview with William F. Buckley Jr.).

Books

  • Eagleton, Terry: How to Read Literature. Yale University Press, 2014. (232 pages) ISBN 9780300205305. (LC classification: PN49 .E254 2013)
  • Reemtsma, Jan Philipp: Was heißt: einen literarischen Text interpretieren? Voraussetzungen und Implikationen des Redens über Literatur. München: C. H. Beck, 2016. (316 pages) ISBN 978-3-406-69098-3.
  • Scholes, Robert: The Crafty Reader. Yale University Press, 2001. (288 pages) ISBN 9780300191547.
    From the publisher's description: Scholes begins with a critique of the New Critical way of reading (“bad for poets and poetry and really terrible for students and teachers of poetry”), using examples of poems by various writers, in particular Edna St. Vincent Millay. He concludes with a consideration of the strengths and weaknesses of the fundamentalist way of reading texts regarded as sacred.
  • Von Lovenberg, Felicitas: Gebrauchsanweisung fürs Lesen. München: Piper, 2018. (128 pages) ISBN 978-3-492-27717-4.
  • Richards, Ivor Armstrong: Principles of Literary Criticism. (1924, second edition 1926) Routledge, 2001. (296 pages) ISBN 9781138123748.
  • Brooks, Cleanth: An Approach to Literature. Baton Rouge: Louisiana State University Press, 1936. (out of print)
  • Lewis, C.S.: An Experiment in Criticism. [1961] Canto Classics. Cambridge University Press, 2012. (152 pages) ISBN 9781107604728.
    According to Wikipedia, Lewis proposes that the quality of books should be measured not by how they are written, but by how they are read. An Experiment in Criticism online.
  • Rawlinson, H. D.: The Practice of Criticism. Cambridge University Press, 1968. (248 pages) ISBN 9780521095402.
  • Rodway, Allan: The Craft of Criticism. Cambridge University Press, 1982. (204 pages) ISBN 9780521299091.
    From the publisher's description of the book: Originally published in 1982, this book attempts to bridge the gap between the theory and practice of criticism. The bulk of the book consists of practical critiques of poems of various kinds from various periods that illustrate a variety of critical methods.
  • Hirsch, Edward: How to Read a Poem: And Fall in Love with Poetry. Mariner / Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2000. (384 pages) ISBN 9780156005661.
  • Candel Bormann, Daniel: Literatur interpretieren. Ein Analyse-Tool. UTB 3852. Göttingen: Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, 2013. (157 pages) ISBN 978-3-8252-3852-0.
  • Foster, Thomas C.: How to Read Novels Like a Professor: A Jaunty Exploration of the World’s Favorite Literary Form. HarperCollins, 2017. (336 pages) ISBN 9780061340406.
  • Foster, Thomas C.: How to Read Poetry Like a Professor: A Quippy and Sonorous Guide to Verse. HarperCollins, 2018. (224 pages) ISBN 9780062113788.
  • Sutherland, John: How to Read a Novel: A User's Guide. St. Martin's Press, 2007. (272 pages) ISBN 9780312359898.
  • Pellat, Jean-Christophe; Caltot, Pierre-Alain; Fonvielle, Stéphanie; Franck, Caroline; Rietz, Valentin: Le Grevisse de l'enseignant - L'analyse des textes. Magnard, 2017. (368 pages) ISBN 9782210106307.
  • Hébert, Louis: L’Analyse des textes littéraires - Une méthodologie complète. Classiques Garnier, 2015. (346 pages) ISBN 978-2-8124-3327-6.
  • Tyson, Lois: Using Critical Theory: How to Read and Write About Literature. Second edition. Routledge, 2011. (350 pages) ISBN 9780415616171.
    From the publisher's description: Explaining both why theory is important and how to use it, Lois Tyson introduces beginning students of literature to this often daunting area in a friendly and approachable style.
  • Jacobs, Richard: A Beginner's Guide to Critical Reading: An Anthology of Literary Texts. Routledge, 2001. (496 pages) ISBN 9780415234689.
    Jacobs presents a number of literary texts from Wyatt and Shakespeare to Bishop and Berryman, with critical commentaries attached to them. The author writes, The commentaries are designed to encourage you to see texts and their contexts as inter-related, and to introduce you to a variety of different ways of approaching literary texts.
  • Pope, Rob: Studying English Literature and Language: An Introduction and Companion. Third edition. Routledge, 2012. (448 pages) ISBN 9780415498760.
    Jacobs (see above) praised the second edition of this book as the most helpful and comprehensive guide to advanced literary study available in one volume.
  • Bennett, Andrew; Royle, Nicholas: An Introduction to Literature, Criticism and Theory. Fifth edition. Routledge, 2016. (425 pages) ISBN 9781138119031.
    According to Jacobs (see above), this book can be used as a complement to Pope's book; it offers cogent and lucid essays on a range of literary topics and includes outstanding reading-lists.
  • Eaglestone, Robert: Doing English: A Guide for Literature Students. Fourth edition. Routledge, 2017. (178 pages) ISBN 9781138039674.
    RIchard Jacobs describes this book as a short and straightforward guide aimed at less confident students.
  • Pugh, Tison; Johnson, Margaret E.: Literary Studies: A Practical Guide. Routledge, 2013. (324 pages) ISBN 9780415536929.
  • Hogan, Patrick Colm: Cognitive Science, Literature, and the Arts: A Guide for Humanists. Routledge, 2003. (272 pages) ISBN 9780415942454.
    An introduction to cognitive poetics.
  • Stockwell, Peter: Cognitive Poetics: An Introduction. Routledge, 2002. (208 pages) ISBN 9780415258951.
  • Gavins, Joanna; Steen, Gerard (editors): Cognitive Poetics in Practice. Routledge, 2003. (204 pages) ISBN 9780415277990.
