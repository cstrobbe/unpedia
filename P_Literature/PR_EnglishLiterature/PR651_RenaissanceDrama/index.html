Editions

  • Webster, John: The Works of John Webster. (3 Volume Paperback Set) Edited by David Gunby, David Carnegie, Antony Hammond, Doreen DelVecchio and Macdonald Jackson. Cambridge University Press, 2008. ISBN 9780521738736.
  • Jonson, Ben: The Cambridge Edition of the Works of Ben Jonson (7 Volume Set) Edited by David Bevington, Martin Butler, Ian Donaldson (and many others). Cambridge University Press, 2012. ISBN 9780521782463.
    See also the online version at universitypublishingonline.org/cambridge/jonson.
  • Beaumont, Francis; Fletcher, John: The Works of Francis Beaumont and John Fletcher. Volume 1, edited by Arnold Glover, Cambridge University Press, 1905. First of ten volumes; scanned version available on Archive.org.

Online Play Texts

  • English Renaissance Drama. A website created by Anniina Jokinen that covers authors from the Tudor, Elizabethan and Jacobean periods, for example, John Heywood (c.1497-c.1580), John Lyly (1554-1606), Christopher Marlowe, Ben Jonson and Thomas Middleton (c.1580-1627).

Introductions and Handbooks

  • Pollard, Tanya (editor): Shakespeare's Theater: A Sourcebook. Wiley-Blackwell, 2004. (388 pages) ISBN 978-1-405-11194-2.
    From the publisher's description: A collection of the most significant Elizabethan and Jacobean texts on the morality of the theater.
  • Charry, Brinda: The Arden Guide to Renaissance Drama: An Introduction with Primary Sources. The Arden Shakespeare. Bloomsbury, 2017. (360 pages) ISBN 9781472572240.
  • The Compendium of Renaissance Drama: a reference work edited by Brian Jay Corrigan (general editor), A. R. Braunmuller, Andrew Gurr, Patrick Spottiswoode, J. L. Styan and Robert F. Wilson, Jr. The sites is hosted by the University of North Georgia, which was not involved in creating the resource. For background, see UNG Libraries presents the Compendium of Renaissance Drama (29.07.2016).
  • Clark, Sandra: Renaissance Drama. Cultural History of Literature Series. Polity Press, 2007. (232 pages) ISBN 9780745633114.
    See also the brief review in Times Higher Education, 18 September 2008.
  • Howard, Jean E.: The Stage and Social Struggle in Early Modern England. Routledge, 1994. (192 pages) ISBN 9780415095532.
  • Wells, Stanley: Shakespeare and Co.. Penguin, 2007. (304 pages) ISBN 9780141017136.
    About Shakespeare and a number of his contemporaries: Christopher Marlowe, Thomas Dekker, Ben Jonson, Thomas Middleton, John Fletcher and others.
