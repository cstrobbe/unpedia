    • Beginner Calisthenics Workout At Home (Full Routine) (Minus the Gym on YouTube, 15 minutes, 22.10.2018).

The pushing movement may be wall pushups, incline pushups, standard pushups or other pushup variations. The hinge exercise may be lying tucks, lying leg raises or hanging leg raises.

Training Advice

  • Le gainage, c'est bien? Parlons en! (Major Mouvement on YouTube, 3 minutes, 31.03.2018.
    This video demonstrates several alternatives to traditional (static) planks: gainage pas de l'ours, gainage latéral (raise one arm above your head while turning your body), gainage tape épaules, gainage pompes coudes, gainage stabilité chevilles, gainage push main push coudes, gainage dead bug, gainage combat and gainage fessiers.
  • 22 Ab Exercises Ranked (Worst to Best!) (Calisthenicmovement on YouTube, 15 minutes, 04.12.2020).
    The exercises were ranked based on abdominal activity and all factors that can influence this: if an exercise requires a lot of mobility or if it relies strongly on other muscles (especially if fatigue in ohter muscles prevents you from cotinuing the exercise), that is used as an argument to rank the exercise lower. This does not imply that you should avoid compound movement to train your abdominals.
    The D rank (worst) has the following exercises: situps (hip flexors, spine), frontlever, planche and the bird dog (which is not bad for absolute beginners). The C rank has “toes to the bar” (requires too much mobility and relies on strong quads), V-up (also requires balance and quad strength), the human flag (requires too much shoulder strength) and windshield wipers (requires a lot of mobility and possibly shearing forces in the lower spine).
    The B rank has the following erercises: the dead bug (good for the lower spine but possibly too easy for intermediate and advanced athletes), mountain climbers, the basic plank (a beginner exercise but static), leg raises (require less mobility than “toes to the bar”), heel taps in a static crunch position (too easy for intermediate athletes), the candle raise, the Russian twist (which includes a rotational components and can be made harder by doing it with the legs off the ground). the dragon flag and the roll-out.
    The A rank has the following exercises: the long lever plank (puts less pressure on shoulders and elbows than the roll-out), (hanging) knee raises, side plan raise (good for targeting the obliques) and the reverse crunch.
    The S rank has just one exercise: the hollow body crunch.
  • Improving Your Reaction Time | Fit for the 500 (IU Health on YouTube, 1 minute, 05.04.2014).
    The first exercise shown in this video consists in throwing a tennis ball at a wall with one hand and catching it with the other (in both directions) to improve eye-hand co-ordination. The other exercises require a partner.
  • Chris Hemsworth's Workout Explained By His Personal Trainer | Train Like a Celebrity (Men's Health on YouTube, 6 minutes, 27.11.2018).
    Luke Zocchi, Chris Hemsworth's personal trainer, explains the workout he designed for Hemsworth for the film Men in Black: Internationalk (2019). The workout was designed to achieve a less bulky look than Thor and to be doable while travelling, so it involves only bodyweight and a pair of dumbbells. It starts with a warm-up that involves bear crawls (10 rounds of 20 seconds, with resting periods of 10 seconds) and bodyweight squats (also 10 rounds of 20 seconds, with resting periods of 10 seconds). The other exercises are weighted burpee (8 reps), curl to press (8 reps), goblet squats (8 reps), standing triceps extensions (8 reps), reverse lunge curls (8 reps), a shoulder excercise that cycles through a lateral raise, a front raise and an upright row (8 reps), and three plank variations: plank punches (8 reps), plank pulses (8 reps) and plank pikes (8 reps). This set of exercises are done in a cycle for thirty minutes.
    Warning: Skip or replace the upright rows in the shoulder exercise; the upright row demonstrated in this video raises the elbows above shoulder level, which brings your shoulders into internal rotation, and this can be harmful to the shoulder joints.

Walking and Fat Loss

  • How I Use Walking To Get Lean (6 Ways) (Mario Tomic on YouTube, 7 minutes, 06.07.2020).
  • How Much Walking To Get To 10% Body Fat (My Walking Routine) (Mario Tomic on YouTube, 7 minutes, 30.11.2020).
    Walking more worked best for Mario Tomic when combined with resistance training. For him, it required 10.000 to 15.000 steps per day, which resulted in 300 calories burnt net per day (net calories refers to the amount of calories burnt by walking minus the amount of calories one would burn while sitting down). He combined this with a caloric deficit in his nutrition (below 2000 calories per day), resulting in a loss of 1.5 pounds per week. He did most of the walking (two thirds) after the workout, in order not to lose too much energy for the workout.
    In order to find out how many steps you need to walk, you should first identify a baseline: how many steps you have been doing before. It you have been doing around 4.000 steps per day, you should set your initial goal to 7.000 or 8.000 and increase your average later. You should make the walking part of your schedule. It is also helpful to set up trackers that alert you when you miss your goal.

  • 3500 Calories in a Pound of Body Fat. Yes and NO (Every Damn Day Fitness on YouTube, 4 minutes, 25.07.2018).
    Alan Roberts often says that a pound of fat corresponds with 3500 calories. The number varies a bit. Research has shown that there are between 8.7 and 9.5 calories in a gram of pure fat, and 9 calories is the generally accepted mean. However, body fat is not pure fat because it also contains fluids, proteins and other things. According to research 87% of body fat cells are pure fat. A simple calculation woud arrive at 3554 calories for a pound of fat, which is rounded down to 3500. (There are 454 grams in a pound, so 9 * 454 * 0.87 = 4086 * 0.87 = 3554.)
    (For those using SI units: there are around 7830 calories in a kilogram of fat.)
