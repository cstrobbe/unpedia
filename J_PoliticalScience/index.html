  • Sartwell, Crispin: The Left-Right Political Spectrum Is Bogus, The Atlantic, 20.06.2014.
    The positions on both sides of the political spectrum are incoherent.

    The left-right divide might be a division between social identities based on class or region or race or gender, but it is certainly not a clash between different political ideas.

    The arrangement of positions along the left-right axis—progressive to reactionary, or conservative to liberal, communist to fascist, socialist to capitalist, or Democrat to Republican—is conceptually confused, ideologically tendentious, and historically contingent. And any position anywhere along it is infested by contradictions.

    For example, conceptualising the left-right division as state versus capital is false, because, accordig to the author, hierarchies tend to coincide.
