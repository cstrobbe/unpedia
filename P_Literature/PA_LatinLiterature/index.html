  • Cicero: Cicero's Letters to His Friends. Translated By D. R. Shackleton Bailey and Foreword by James E. G. Zetzel. Society for Classical Studies Classical Resources. Oxford University Press, 1989. (888 pages) ISBN 9781555402648.
  • Cicero: Defence Speeches. Translated by D. H. Berry. Oxford World's Classics. Oxford University Press, 2008. (320 pages) ISBN 9780199537907.
    Cicero's most famous defence speeches: of Roscius, falsely accused of murdering his father; of the consul-elect Murena, accused of electoral bribery; of the poet Archias, on a citizenshiup charge; of Caelius, ex-lover of Clodia Metelli, on charges of violence; and of Milo, for mudering Cicero's hated enemy Clodius.
