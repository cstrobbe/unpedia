Classical Chinese Literature

  • Wikipedia: Chinese literature: Classical fiction and drama.
  • Wikipedia: Classic Chinese Novels.
  • Wikipedia: Romance of the Three Kingdoms (14th century, attributed to Luo Guanzhong).
    Translations:
    • A translation with footnotes by Yu Sumei, edited by Ronald C. Iverson, was published by Tuttle in 2014:
      • The Three Kingdoms, Volume 1: The Sacred Oath. Tuttle, May 2014. (416 pages) ISBN 9780804843935.
      • The Three Kingdoms, Volume 2: The Sleeping Dragon. Tuttle, May 2014. (448 pages) ISBN 9780804843942.
      • The Three Kingdoms, Volume 3: Welcome The Tiger. Tuttle, May 2014. (512 pages) ISBN 9780804843959.
    • The University of California Press and the Foreign Languages Press in Beijing published a translation by Moss Roberts:
      • Three Kingdoms, A Historical Novel: Part 1. University of California Press, 2004. (552 pages) ISBN 9780520224780.
      • Three Kingdoms, A Historical Novel: Part 2. University of California Press, 2004. (560 pages) ISBN 9780520225039.
    • Tuttle also still sells the older translation by C. H. Brewitt-Taylor, written in the 1920s and based on a shortened version of the original novel. This translation was republished with an introduction by Robert E. Hegel.
      • The Romance of the Three Kingdoms, Volume 1. Tuttle, 2002. (708 pages) ISBN 9780804834674.
      • The Romance of the Three Kingdoms, Volume 2. Tuttle, 2002. (680 pages) ISBN 9780804834681.
    See also Romance of the Three Kingdoms Podcast.
  • Wikipedia: Water Margin (story set in the Song dynasty; written in the 14th century, attributed to Shi Nai'an).
  • Wikipedia: Journey to the West (story set in the Tang dynasty; written in the 16th century, attributed to Wu Cheng'en). See also:
    • Anthony C. Yu's translation:
      • Xuanzang: The Journey to the West, Volume 1. Translated and Edited by Anthony C. Yu. Revised edition. University of Chicago Press, 2012. (576 pages) ISBN 9780226971322.
      • Xuanzang: The Journey to the West, Volume 2. Translated and Edited by Anthony C. Yu. Revised edition. University of Chicago Press, 2012. (432 pages) ISBN 9780226971346.
      • Xuanzang: The Journey to the West, Volume 3. Translated and Edited by Anthony C. Yu. Revised edition. University of Chicago Press, 2012. (440 pages) ISBN 9780226971377.
      • Xuanzang: The Journey to the West, Volume 4. Translated and Edited by Anthony C. Yu. Revised edition. University of Chicago Press, 2012. (448 page) ISBN 9780226971391.
      • The Monkey and the Monk: An Abridgment of The Journey to the West. Translated by Anthony C. Yu. University of Chicago Press, 2006. (528 pages) ISBN 9780226971568.
    • Xuanzang: The Great Tang Dynasty Record of the Western Regions. Translated by Li Rongxi. University of Hawai'i Press, 2006. (426 pages) ISBN 9781886439023.
    • A Supplement to the Journey to the West, (written around 1640) which does not have the same status as Journey to the West. A translation of this supplement was published under the title The Tower of Myriad Mirrors: A Supplement to Journey to the West. Volume 1 by the University of Michigan Press (2000, ISBN 978-0-89264-142-0).
  • Wikipedia: Jin Ping Mei (written during the late Ming dynasty).
  • Wikipedia: Dream of the Red Chamber (also known as The Story of the Stone; written in the middle of the 18th century by Cao Xueqin).
  • Wikipedia: The Scholars (story set in the Ming dynasty; written in the 18th century by Wu Jingzi).
