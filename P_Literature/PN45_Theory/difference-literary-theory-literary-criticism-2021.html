What Is the Difference Between Literary Theory and Literary Criticism?

Literary Theory Versus Literary Criticism

You know I can't normally tell them apart.

Wikipedia defines literary theory as the systematic study of the nature of literature and of the methods for literary analysis and literary criticism as the study, evaluation, and interpretation of literature.

In Chapter 1 of Literary Theory: A Very Short Introduction 1997), Jonathan Culler wrote that people complained that there was too much theory in literary studies (pages 1–2):

What they have in mind may be precisely that there is too much discussion of non-literary matters, too much debate about general questions whose relation to literature is scarcely evident, too much reading of difficult psychoanalytical, political, and philosophical texts.

Culler also says (page 3),

Theory in literary studies is not an account of the nature of literature or methods for its study (though such matters are part of theory and will be treated here, …). It's a body of thinking and writing whose limits are exceedingly hard to define.
