Learning Haskell

  • Pluralsight: Why should I learn Haskell? (YouTube, 4 minutes, 25.10.2013). This video is mainly about the history of Haskell, which spent a long incubation time in research labs.
  • Coding Simplified: Why is Haskell AWESOME? Explained in 6 Minutes (YouTube, 6 minutes, 22.03.2014). This video is about Haskell's main characteristics and how these differ from other languages: abstraction, the absence of side effects, lazy execution and strong typing. The video is based on the article Why Haskell Matters but is less detailed.
  • Learn Haskell in 10 minutes (on the Haskell wiki).
  • Lipovaca, Miran: Learn You a Haskell for Great Good! A Beginner's Guide is an introduction to Haskell that can be bought on paper or read online.
  • Allen, Chris [bitemyapp]: Learn Haskell: a recommended path for learning Haskell. This has been translated into several other languages. The content is available under the terms of the Creative Commons Zero v1.0 Universal licence.
  • Allen, Chris: Modeling Data in Haskell for Beginners (YouTube, 98 minutes, 30.12.2015). Video of a presentation at LambdaConf 2015.
  • Allen, Christopher; Moronuki, Julie: Haskell Programming from First Principles (book that can be bought online only; a few sample chapters can be downloaded for free).
  • Hutton, Graham: Programming in Haskell. Second edition. Cambridge University Press, 2016. (318 pages) ISBN 9781316626221.
  • Bird, Richard: Thinking Functionally with Haskell. Cambridge University Press, 2014. (354 pages) ISBN 9781107452640.
  • Thompson, Simon: Haskell: The Craft of Functional Programming. Third edition. Addison-Wesley Professional, 2011. (608 pages) ISBN 978-0-201-88295-7.
  • Hudak, Paul: The Haskell School of Expression: Learning Functional Programming through Multimedia. Cambridge University Press, 2000. (382 pages) ISBN 9780521644082.
  • O'Sullivan, Bryan; Stewart, Don; Goerzen, John: Real World Haskell.
  • School of Haskell (sponsored by FP Complete).
  • Introduction to Haskell, FP Complete.
  • Haskell, Wikibooks: tutorial in English.
  • Funktionale Programmierung mit Haskell, Wikibooks: tutorial in German (work in progress).
  • Davie, Antony J. T.: Introduction to Functional Programming Systems Using Haskell. Cambridge University Press, 1992. (304 pages) ISBN 9780521277242.
  • Functional Programming in Haskell: Supercharge Your Coding: online course by the University of Glasgow hosted on FutureLearn. The course is six weeks long and requires four hours of study per week. The most recent session started on 2 April 2018.
  • Banas, Derek: Haskell Tutorial: Learn Haskell in One Video (YouTube, 77 minutes, 01.08.2015).

Haskell Libraries and Software

  • Hackage: the Haskell community's central package archive of open source software.
  • Leksah: an open-source Haskell IDE writtin in Haskell. The programs runs on Linux, Windows and Mac OS X. The project has a source code repository on GitHub.
  • Yi: The Haskell-Scriptable Editor: a collection of packages that serve as building blocks for making your very own text editor.
  • O'Sullivan, Bryan: wreq: a Haskell web client library: available under the BSD licence.
  • Hakyll: a Haskell library for generating static sites.

Other Haskell Links

  • Recommended Reading: video from the online course Funtional Programming in Haskell by the University of Glasgow. However, the book recommendations are from Simon Thompson from the University of Kent.
  • Horton, Nick: How To Install The Haskell Platform On Arch Linux (or Manjaro, Antergos, Archbang, etc), Sapien Games, 05.04.2014.
  • How to install Haskell (Platform or Stack) in 2018 on Linux? (a question on Stack Overflow). See also How to install Haskell (Platform or Stack) in 2018 on Linux?, A Passionate Techie, 12.02.2018.
  • Allen, Chris: How to Learn Haskell in Less Than 5 Years (YouTube, 42 minutes, 04.01.2015): video of a presentation at the LambdaConf in May 2015. This video is not a Haskell tutorial. Allen says, among other things, that he learnt Haskell by working through exercises and working on his own projects, and that bouncing between different resources cost him a lot of time.
  • Jones, Simon Peyton: Escape from the ivory tower: the Haskell journey (YouTube, 64 minutes, 25.04.2017): video of a talk at Churchill College, University of Cambridge in March 2007 by one of the inventors of Haskell.
  • Jones, Simon Peyton, et al: Haskell 98 Language and Libraries: The Revised Report, December 2002.
  • Snoyman, Michael: What Makes Haskell Unique (YouTube, 42 minutes, 04.01.2018): video of a presentation at the f(by) 17 Conference in Functional Programming.
  • Miller, Katie: Haskell is Not For Production and Other Tales (YouTube, 38 minutes, 06.02.2016): video of a presentation at linux.conf.au in Geelong, Australia in January 2016.
  • Stack Overflow: Haskell.
  • Gonzalez, Gabriel: Haskell for all: a blog with content available under a Creative Commons licence.
  • Lynn, Ben: Haskell Sorcery, Ben Lynn's website (no date).
  • Klyne, Graham: Learning Haskell Notes (probably not updated since 2005 or 2008).
  • Henrichs, Rein: Haskell Live: A Live Coding Adventure. Haskell Live is a free (as in beer), creative-commons attribution licensed series of live coding videos that teach basic to intermediate Haskell concepts. Henrichs also describes his programming environment for Haskell, which is based on tmux, several vim plugins, and the Ruby-based tools guard and guard-shell. The source code from the episodes is available on GitHub but has not been updated since 2012.
