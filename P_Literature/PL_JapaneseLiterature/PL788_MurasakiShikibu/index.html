Reviews and Literature about The Tale of Genji

  • Harper, Thomas; Shirane, Haruo: Reading The Tale of Genji: Sources from the First Millennium. Columbia University Press, 2015. (632 pages) ISBN 9780231166584 (hardcover).
    From the publisher's book description: (…)This sourcebook is the most comprehensive record of the reception of The Tale of Genji to date. It presents a range of landmark texts relating to the work during its first millennium, almost all of which are translated into English for the first time. An introduction prefaces each set of documents, situating them within the tradition of Japanese literature and cultural history. (…)
  • Puette, William J.: The Tale of Genji: A Reader's Guide. Tuttle Publishing, 2009. (192 pages) ISBN 9784805310847.
  • McCormick, Melissa: The Tale of Genji: A Visual Companion. Princeton University Press, 2018. (288 pages) ISBN 9780691172682.
    See also Sarah Rose Sharp's review A Close Look at the Art of Genji (Hyperallergic, 18.02.2019).
  • McMullen, James: Murasaki Shikibu's The Tale of Genji: Philosophical Perspectives. Oxford Studies in Philosophy and Literature. Oxford University Press, 2019. (288 pages) ISBN 9780190654986.
  • Shirane, Haruo (editor): Envisioning The Tale of Genji: Media, Gender, and Cultural Production. Columbia University Press, 2008. (416 pages) ISBN 9780231142373.
  • Buruma, Ian: The Sensualist: What makes “The Tale of Genji” so seductive, The New Yorker, 13.07.2015.
    A review of Dennis Washburn's translation.
  • Bargen, Doris G.: The Search for Things Past in The Genji monogatari, Harvard Journal of Asiatic Studies, Vol. 51, No. 1 (June 1991), pp. 199-232.
  • Eustacia: The Tale of Genji by Murasaki Shikibu translated and abridged by Edward G. Seidensticker, Eustea Reads, 14.12.2018.
  • Eustacia: The Tale of Genji (Unabridged Version) translated by Arthur Waley, Eustea Reads, 18.03.2019.
  • Eustacia: The Tale of Genji: A Reader’s Guide by William J. Puette, Eustea Reads, 20.03.2019.
  • Eustacia: What I’ve learned from papers about The Tale of Genji, Eustea Reads, 10.05.2019.
  • Eustacia: Wearing Your Heart on Your Sleeve: Meaning in Uniqlo’s Tale of Genji Shirts, Eustea Reads, 21.03.2020.
