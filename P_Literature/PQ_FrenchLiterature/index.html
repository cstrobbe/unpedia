Michel Eyquem de Montaigne (1533—1592)

Montaigne's Works

  • Essais/édition de Bordeaux, 1595, hosted on Wikisource.
  • Journal du Voyage de Michel de Montaigne en Italie, par la Suisse et l’Allemagne, en 1580 et 1581, digitised version of an edition published in 1774, hosted on Wikisource.
  • Montaigne, Les Essais - The Montaigne Project: this site, hosted by the University of Chicago Library, provides a digitised version of the Villey editon of Les Essais, which can also be accessed using a search engine.
  • Montaigne: Essayes of Montaigne. Volume 1. Translated by John Florio. London: Gibbings & Company: 1603. Scanned version of an 1890 reprint on Archive.org. This volume contains 26 essays, including the following: “By diverse meanes men come unto a like end”, “Of sadnesse or sorrowe”, “Our affections are transported beyond our selves”, “How the soule dischargeth her passions upon false objects, when the true faile it”, “Whether the Captaine of a place besieged ought to sallie forth to parlie”, “That the houre of parlies is dangerous”, “That our intention judgeth our actions”, “Of Idlenesse”, “Of Lyers”, “Of readie or slow Speech”, “Of Prognostications”, “Of Constancie”, “Of Ceremonies in the enterview of Kings”.
  • Montaigne: Essayes of Montaigne. Volume 2. Translated by John Florio. London: Gibbings & Company: 1603. Scanned version of an 1890 reprint on Archive.org. This volume contains 31 essays, including the following: “Of Friendship”, “Of Moderation”, “Of the Canniballes”, “Of Cato the younger”, “Of Solitarinesse”, “Of Age”.
  • Montaigne: Essayes of Montaigne. Volume 3. Translated by John Florio. London: Gibbings & Company: 1603. Scanned version of an 1890 reprint on Archive.org. This volume contains the following essays: “Of the Inconstancie of our Actions”, “Of Drunkennesse”, “Of Custome of the Ile of Cea”, “To Morrow is a New Day”, “Of Conscience”, “Of Exercise or Practice”, “Of the Recompences or Rewards of Honour”, “Of the Affections of Fathers to their Children. To the Lady of Estissac”, “Of the Parthians Armes”, “Of Bookes”, “Of Cruelti”, “An Apologie of Raymond Sebond”.

Jean Giono (1895—1970)

  • Jean Giono, écrivain de la violence humaine (France Culture on YouTube, 4 minutes, 23.10.2019).
    Excerpts from an interview with novelist Emmanuelle Lambert, who published a book about Giono. Giono's work is often thought of as an example of régionnalisme, but the author rejected that type of literature. Lambert thinks of him more as an author about human violence.
