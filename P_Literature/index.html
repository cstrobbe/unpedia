Text Collections and Archives

  • Perseus Digital Library: a digital library with collections of Greek and Roman materials, Arabic language documents, Germanic materials, Renaissance materials and 19th-century American materials.
  • The Virtual Library: a collection of texts that are in the public domain.
  • Project Gutenberg: collection of over 55,000 free e-books.
  • Internet Sacred Text Archive.
  • Representative Poetry Online (RPO): an anthology of poems in English and French by over 700 poets. This includes HTML versions of manuscripts of Geoffrey Chaucer's works.
  • The Digital Humanities Institute maintains website that include the following:
    • The Acts and Monuments Online: several editions of a work that is also known as the Book of Martyrs.
    • Manuscripts Online
    • The Hartlib Papers
    • Torquemada en la hoguera
    • The Sheffield Corpus of Chinese: texts from the Song (960-1279), Ming (1366-1644) and Qing (1644-1911) dynasties.
  • Textes édités à ATHENA (©): collection of texts in various languages (Latin, French, German and Spanish but mostly in French).
  • Sources littéraires - E-Textes - Quelques archives générales: links to archives of electronic texts.

Online Courses

  • Modern Masterpieces of World Literature is a course by Harvard University hosted on edX. It is an 8-week course that requires 3 to 6 hours of study per week.
  • Ancient Masterpieces of World Literature is a course by Harvard University hosted on edX. It is an six-week course that requires 3 to 6 hours of study per week.
  • 唐宋词鉴赏 | Introduction to Ci Poems in the Tang and Song Dynasty is a course by Tsinghua University (Beijing, China) hosted on edX. It is an 8-week course that required 3 to 4 hours of study per week. It is part of a series of “Appreciation of Tang and Song Poetry” and is entirely in Chinese.
